## 2. The Digital Mechanism Enables Retroactive Rewriting

### How Physical Media Preserved Truth (Imperfectly)

**Pre-digital knowledge storage:**
- Books printed and distributed (decentralized, persistent)

**Example:** Even totalitarian regimes with book-burning campaigns could not erase all records. Samizdat literature, hidden manuscripts, scattered copies preserved suppressed truths.

**Current knowledge storage:**
- Information on centralized servers (cloud, platforms, databases)
- To alter history: edit database, update search indexes, retrain AI models
- Process instant (seconds to propagate)
- Process complete (all instances update if centralized)
- Search algorithms determine what's findable

**Examples of active retroactive rewriting:**

**Wikipedia:**

**Search engine manipulation:**
- Rankings shift (certain sources buried, others promoted)
- Autocomplete guides toward approved narratives
- "Related searches" shape what seems relevant
- What's unfindable becomes effectively nonexistent

**E-book updates:**
- Publishers remotely update content (including Orwell's *1984*, ironically)
- Readers unaware text changed
- No persistent version under user control

**Social media deletion:**
- Platforms remove posts retroactively (terms of service)
- Accounts suspended (entire history vanishes)
- API access revoked (researchers can't verify claims)
- Past disappears if platform decides

**Community verification:**
- Peer witnesses (those who encountered work early)
- Relationship testimony (collaborators confirm precedence)
