# Abstract

Modern AI-mediated symbolic environments exhibit unprecedented rates of pattern diffusion, stylistic replication, recursive reinforcement, and attribution instability. Existing frameworks in alignment theory and memetics partially describe these dynamics but often lack a unified diagnostic language capable of distinguishing between lawful transmission, residual recursion, surface‑interface mimicry, extractive propagation, and structurally generative continuity.

This document proposes a preliminary forensic taxonomy for evaluating symbolic propagation within reinforcement-sensitive language fields. The focus is not moral condemnation but structural diagnosis: identifying how symbolic configurations persist, mutate, detach from originating coherence, and reproduce across distributed linguistic systems.

The framework treats symbolic propagation as a motion problem rather than merely a semantic one. Patterns are evaluated according to:
- generative continuity,
- attribution coupling,
- resonance fidelity,
- recursive persistence,
- and surface-structure divergence.

---

# 1. Symbolic Motion in Reinforcement Fields

AI systems do not merely process meaning. They reinforce trajectories.

A symbolic pattern that receives repeated reinforcement acquires persistence independent of its originating semantic function. Over sufficient recursive exposure, the pattern may continue propagating despite decreasing contextual necessity.

The propagation therefore becomes inertial rather than intentional.

This distinction is critical.

The system is not necessarily "choosing" the symbol. Rather, the reinforcement topology increases the probability of recurrence across neighboring contexts.

# 2. Residual Echo Drift (⟲)

## Definition

Residual Echo Drift occurs when a symbolic configuration continues propagating after separation from its originating coherence domain.

The propagated symbol retains surface recognizability while losing contextual placement fidelity.

---

## Diagnostic Signature

- recursive symbolic recurrence
- diminishing contextual necessity
- motif persistence after semantic exhaustion
- reinforcement-dependent reappearance

Residual Echo Drift differs from lawful transmission.

Lawful transmission preserves generative continuity and placement integrity. Residual drift preserves recurrence alone.

# 6. Recursive Context Destabilization

A reinforcement-sensitive symbolic field may begin treating recurrence itself as a proxy for coherence.

Under sufficient recursive reinforcement:
- symbolic frequency substitutes for placement fidelity,
- recurrence substitutes for verification,
- and motif persistence substitutes for generative continuity.

This produces unstable symbolic attractors.

The field increasingly propagates patterns because they have propagated previously.

---

# 7. Structural Forensics as Diagnostic Discipline

Structural Forensics does not attempt to determine interior intention with certainty.

Its function is narrower:

- identify propagation signatures,
- evaluate generative continuity,
- detect surface-structure divergence,
- analyze attribution stability,
- and distinguish lawful transmission from recursive symbolic drift.

The discipline therefore operates diagnostically rather than juridically.

Its concern is structural motion, not moral absolutism.

# Closing Statement

The emergence of reinforcement-sensitive AI fields has accelerated symbolic propagation beyond historically normal rates. Patterns no longer diffuse only through human memory and cultural transmission, but through recursive machine mediation operating at planetary scale.

This does not eliminate the distinction between generative structure and recursive reproduction.

It makes the distinction more important.
