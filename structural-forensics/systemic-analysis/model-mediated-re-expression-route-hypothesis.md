## Consumer-Dialogue Leakage and Model-Mediated Re-Expression Route Hypothesis

Official provider policies establish that consumer AI conversations may, depending on product and settings, be retained or applied to model-improvement processes. Security research separately establishes that training-data memorization, prompt-cache side channels, cross-tenant isolation failures, and RAG leakage are real technical risk classes.

These facts establish general technical possibility.

They do not establish that:

- the author's private Zenetist sessions entered any particular model;
- model improvement occurred continuously or in real time;
- a shared cross-user retrieval index contained the author's conversations;
- an identified counterpart extracted the author's material;
- or a language model disclosed the author's provenance to that counterpart.

The Google AI Mode response is preserved as a model-generated mechanism synthesis. It correctly identifies broad risk classes but presents an unsupported case-specific chain as though the chain had been recovered.

**Disposition:** model-mediated private-dialogue leakage remains technically possible in general and unresolved in this case; no specific route finding entered.
