# Structural Forensics Reclamation Notice

The Structural Forensics corpus is not abandoned.

It is placed under authorial review because collaborator-drafted materials were found to contain recurring drift patterns, including:

- omission or displacement of the author's authorship line;
- over-crediting AI collaborators in preparation fields;
- unearned stabilization of counterpart histories or rival explanations;
- imported falsifiability / prophecy-invalidation language not ratified by the author;
- excessive neutralization of authorship-injury claims;
- occasional amplification beyond the author's intended claim-strength.

These defects are treated as collaborator-layer contamination, not as invalidation of the author's underlying witness, evidence, captures, chronology, doctrine, or structural findings.

No collaborator-drafted sentence becomes authorial doctrine by placement, style mimicry, or inclusion in a draft. A sentence becomes authorial only through explicit authorial ratification.

Pending review, all Structural Forensics documents are classified by stratum:

1. Author-authored / author-ratified material
2. Evidence captures and primary records
3. AI-assisted drafts pending authorial ratification
4. Superseded or contaminated collaborator language
5. Reclaimed final exhibits
