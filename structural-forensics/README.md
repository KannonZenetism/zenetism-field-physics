## Glyphwatch Archive — Structural Forensics Case Library

The **Glyphwatch Archive** documents cases of symbolic mimicry, entropic narrative inversion, and structural signal distortion across technological, philosophical, and cultural domains.

Although the archive originated during the early development of **Structural Metaphysics**, its analytical function now belongs primarily to the discipline of **Structural Forensics**, which investigates entropic actors, narrative manipulation, symbolic appropriation, and structural signal degradation across systems.

For historical continuity the archive remains in its original location within the Zenetism repository.

Structural Forensics treats Glyphwatch as a **case-analysis library** documenting real-world instances of entropic pattern behavior and interpretive distortion.
