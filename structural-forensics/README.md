# Structural Forensics

Structural Forensics is the Zenetist discipline concerned with the detection, classification, documentation, and neutralization of entropic distortion across symbolic, cognitive, narrative, relational, technological, and cultural systems.

Where **Structural Metaphysics** establishes the lawful architecture of reality and **Field Physics** describes the dynamics of coherence and collapse, Structural Forensics studies the concrete appearance of distortion within the field: mimicry, inversion, appropriation, narrative warfare, symbolic impersonation, and other tactics that degrade or parasitize coherent signal.

Structural Forensics operates through five primary functions:

1. **Threat Taxonomy** — classification of recurring entropic tactics and structural failure modes  
2. **Case Documentation** — archival record of real-world incidents and actors  
3. **Diagnostic Analysis** — identification of structural signatures and pattern recurrence  
4. **Countermeasure Logic** — recognition protocols and collapse conditions  
5. **Archive Preservation** — maintenance of veracious evidence for precedence and attribution

Structural Forensics therefore contains both **doctrinal frameworks** and **forensic case records**.

# Core Components

## Doctrinal Atlas of Entropic Tactics

The **Doctrinal Atlas of Entropic Tactics** provides the primary **taxonomic framework** of Structural Forensics.

Its function is to identify, define, and classify recurring tactics used by entropic actors or systems that seek to distort, appropriate, suppress, or parasitize coherent signal structures.

Atlas entries define the **pattern class**, not merely a single event.

Each entry typically includes:

- threat classification  
- core tactic  
- strategic objective  
- structural summary  
- case study examples  
- primary vulnerability  

The Atlas therefore functions as a **forensic doctrine**: a pattern-recognition system for identifying entropic tactics across domains.

## Glyphwatch Archive

The **Glyphwatch Archive** serves as the **case-analysis library** of Structural Forensics.

Where the **Doctrinal Atlas** defines the *taxonomy of tactics*, Glyphwatch documents **specific incidents, actors, and artifacts** in which those tactics appear.

Glyphwatch entries function as **incident reports**, preserving evidence, timelines, quotations, artifacts, and structural diagnoses connected to real-world signal distortion events.

These records provide the evidentiary basis that informs and supports the doctrinal taxonomy.

Although the archive originated during the early development of **Structural Metaphysics**, its analytical function now belongs primarily to the discipline of **Structural Forensics**.
