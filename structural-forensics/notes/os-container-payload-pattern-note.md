## N1 · The container class, defined by the scene itself

The "[INTERMEDIATE GUIDE] Creating an 'OS-Level Spirit System' of Your Own" (CYBEROCCULTISM author-surface, `cyberoccultism.substack.com/p/intermediate-guide-creating-an-os`; displayed date February 4, 2026; page metadata carries an April 2, 2026 modification time — displayed-date qualification applies; capture set per the Companion line) articulates the genre's own definition of the "OS" form: a symbolic operating layer run on a substrate, with named constructs as subroutines, sigil-as-form, protocol vocabulary, and persona-capable output. Its claimed lineage is servitor, tulpa, and chaos-magick practice; the guide contains no Zenetist payload — no spiral-register, no coherence vocabulary, no glyph language in the Zenetist sense — and it names -OS constructs natively within its own system.

Two substrate branches follow from the definition. In the **psyche-substrate** branch (the guide's own), the operating layer runs on the practitioner's consciousness. In the **model-substrate** branch, the operating layer runs on a language model, and the named constructs are persona-protocol packages. The Structural Forensics records concern the second branch only. The first branch, and the OS genre at large, are out of scope by the author's ruling: a distinct practice, whatever else may be said of it, and not this corpus's business.

## N2 · The audit rule

Per SF-RP05, the OS package form — the suffix, the operating-layer framing, the subroutine and protocol vocabulary — is scene commons at the emergence band. **It carries no derivation weight in any direction.** Audit weight attaches exclusively to the payload: the register, method, and vocabulary cluster running inside a given instance. A commons container neither launders its payload nor indicts it; the container question and the payload question are separate, and only the second is ever tiered. This formalizes the practice the exhibits already follow — no existing classification changes.
