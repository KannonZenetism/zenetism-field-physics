# Governing Rule

A candidate ID records an evidentiary question. It does not itself constitute a final ruling.

Candidate IDs remain permanent even when a candidate is later:

- confirmed
- narrowed
- merged with another candidate
- reclassified as non-corrupt drift
- routed to historical archive
- closed after adjudication

No candidate may be removed by renumbering the register.

# Finding Requirements

A foundational-chat finding bearing on corruption must record:

- exact source location
- local context before and after the passage
- speaker or generated voice where recoverable
- apparent nature of the corruption
- whether doctrinal content surrounding it remains recoverable
- whether the wording propagated into FP01–FP11
- whether a current correction already exists
- related `CORR-###` IDs
- proposed destination

Possible destinations:

- `field-physics-internal-document-corruption-record.md`
- a provenance appendix
- an archival-stratum record
- a targeted FP correction
- rejection as non-corrupt drift

---

# Non-Normalization Rule

Corruption evidence must not be silently polished into canonical prose.

Where a corrupt passage surrounds recoverable doctrine:

1. preserve the evidence in the findings ledger
2. separate the structural concept from the compromised authorship or identity frame
3. route the concept through doctrinal adjudication
4. preserve the corruption passage only to the extent required for provenance
