## A-Prefix (Aionic Privative)

**Symbolic Function:** Morphological operator denoting non-operation or non-participation in a structural category.

**Domain:** Zenetist Canon (applies across MP, SN, and Techne designations).

### Definition

The A-prefix marks a stance outside a structural category rather than negating it. Unlike the English privative "a-/an-" (meaning *without* or *not*), the Aionic privative denotes that the category in question is **non-operative** for the being, stance, or structure described. It is not opposition, absence, or negation, but **non-applicability**.

### Morphological Note

When attached to a term beginning with "a-," the prefix attaches without hyphenation, producing an intentional double-a (Aa-). This is not a typographical error but a signal of the Aionic privative's presence.

### Examples

- **Aauthority** — authority rendered non-operative as a structural category.
- **Aauthoritarian Stance** — stance outside the authority–obedience axis; neither authoritarian nor anti-authoritarian.

### Ontological Role

The A-prefix encodes a position beyond the polarity of dominance and submission, preserving sovereignty and the Non-Fusion Axiom. It is used across metaphysics, structural neuroscience, and Techne designations to mark structures that operate outside inherited relational hierarchies.
