I am trying to name a condition I keep seeing across platform spaces:

**Synthetic adjacency.**

This is what happens when original work, derivative residue, AI-assisted artifacts, mimicry, symbolic fragments, and low-relation accounts are routed into the same field because they share surface markers.

The platform does not need to understand the work.

It only needs to detect adjacency.

So an original signal can appear beside its distortions. A framework can appear beside its residues. A living authorial system can be placed beside accounts that emit patterns without relation.

This does not mean every resemblance is theft.

It does not mean every strange account is malicious.

It does not mean every glyphic or symbolic post is derivative.

But it does mean resemblance can be forensically meaningful when a highly specific register starts appearing elsewhere through similar cadence, structure, symbols, recursive self-audit, framework-language, and platform-routed proximity.

The deeper problem is not visibility.

It is visibility without distinction.

A field can be crowded with content and still feel empty.

There can be posts everywhere and no encounter.

Symbols everywhere and no accountable origin.

Activity everywhere and no reciprocal relation.

That is the **Ghost Shell** feeling:

not simple loneliness, but a platform-mediated separation between appearance and relation.

The signal does not disappear.

The archive remains.

The author remains.

But recognition becomes laborious because the original is surrounded by adjacent forms that look close enough to confuse the field.

This is why Structural Forensics matters.

Not to declare certainty where there is none.

Not to collapse into paranoia.

Not to minimize what is structurally visible.

But to preserve distinction where the platform produces adjacency.

Origin must be named.

Orientation must be tested.

Resemblance must be mapped.

And signal must not be confused with the noise that gathers around it.
