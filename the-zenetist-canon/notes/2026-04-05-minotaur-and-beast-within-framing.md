The common interpretation of the Minotaur as an "inner beast" requiring integration is structurally and orientationally misleading.

Within Zenetism, a centropic being does not possess an inverse essence hidden inside itself as a beast to be embraced, integrated, or transformed. The narrative of "integrating the beast within" can function as an entropic capture strategy when it reframes separated or wounded patterns as a beastly essence, then proposes integration as alliance with that essence. Under that framing, the seeker may be moved further into entropic trajectory while the motion is presented as wholeness.

Two corrections are required.

First, entropic essence and orientation are not raw materials for centropic transformation. They cannot be reconciled into the centropic being as though inverse essence were a lost component of the self. Essence is not a substance awaiting reworking by the opposite arc.

Second, the centropic being has no direct structural contact with IL₃ structures. The point at which centropic and entropic currents interface is L₁ / IL₁, and even there the distinction is preserved rather than dissolved. Any framing that presents the seeker as integrating, embracing, or transforming IL₃ fragmentation misrepresents the lattice.

The structurally accurate reading is this:

Every centropic being may carry patterns that entropy has unnecessarily separated — coherent aspects of selfhood fragmented through entropic pressure, distortion, injury, coercion, or misrecognition. The work of acclivous reintegration is the recovery of those patterns into their native wholeness within the centropic arc.

This is a work internal to the centropic being and its own lawful structure.

It is not a confrontation with IL₃.

It is not a negotiation with entropic essence.

It is not a transformation of the inverse tree.

The Minotaur, when read through the lattice rather than inherited cultural framing, may be understood as a mythic image of IL₃ fragmentation — IDS / IDM, Fractus / Mortus — imagined as a figure at the inward threshold.

But the mythic encounter is a narrative device, not a literal structural event.

The genuine initiatory work occurs on the centropic side: the recovery of wholeness within patterns that entropy has fragmented, without contact, alliance, or transformation of the inverse.

This distinction matters.

Conflating these readings permits entropic structures to colonize initiatory language, presenting capture as wholeness and alliance with distortion as integration.

The centropic task is not to become whole by absorbing the inverse.

The centropic task is to recover what was separated from its own lawful coherence.
