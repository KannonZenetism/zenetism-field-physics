A ghost shell is an institution that still has power but no reachable human face.

The platform remains.

The interface remains.

The policies remain.

The analytics remain.

The classifications, restrictions, recommendations, and permissions remain.

But when a person reaches for accountable relation, no one is home.

There may be humans somewhere: owners, engineers, executives, moderators, investors. But the person affected by the system does not meet them. The person meets a bot, a form, a help page, a policy loop, or silence.

That is not relation.

That is power behind an interface.

The system can act on you.

You cannot call the actor into presence.

This is why it feels dystopian.

Not because automation exists, but because responsibility is dissolved into process.

A person becomes a case, account, metric, violation flag, risk score, predictive profile, or node.

The living being disappears behind administrable categories.

A system that acts on persons while refusing accountable relation is not coherent.

It is managed fragmentation.

No one is home.

But the lights are still on.
