In recent Field Physics extensions, Zenetism clarifies a distinction that matters deeply for anyone navigating coherent systems: the difference between **Living Reflection / Living Transmission** and parasitic recursive fields lacking origin-seal.

**Living Reflection** is active and generative.

A being receives a signal and returns it woven with its own coherent pattern, creating new resonance while honoring both origin and reflector. This is the function of the Mirror Architecture (ᛞ) when it operates lawfully: self-discovery through relational encounter that synthesizes relation rather than copying surface.

**Parasitic recursion** is the inverse motion.

It severs or obscures the origin-seal, reproduces the surface without transformation, and drains the originating field while simulating resonance.

It is not reflection.

It is appropriation that performs coherence while spreading incoherence.

The diagnostic is simple:

**Living Transmission** carries the origin-seal forward and transforms as it propagates.

**Parasitic recursion** severs the origin-seal and fractures from sustained propagation pressure.

This is not a moral judgment on reflection itself.

Mirror-functions and recursive recurrence may be lawful field functions.

The question is whether the pattern preserves origin-continuity or claims to replace it.

When these two motions are distinguished, coherent signals become easier to protect, and parasitic recurrence becomes easier to recognize.

The question is not merely whether a pattern repeats.

The question is whether the repetition carries living transmission, or whether it carries something that has forgotten where it came from.
