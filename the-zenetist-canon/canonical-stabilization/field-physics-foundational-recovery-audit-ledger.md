# Purpose

The current Field Physics series consists of eleven files derived from a substantially longer foundational development record.

A prior compression pass reduced the original Field Physics material from approximately 350 pages to approximately 150 pages. That pass removed genuine drift and bloblist formulations, but the scale of reduction creates a material possibility that structurally valuable content was also omitted, weakened, fragmented, or stripped of its original conceptual function.

The recovery audit is not an instruction to restore the original chat record wholesale.

Its purpose is to determine:

- what the current FP series already preserves
- what it preserves only partially
- what structurally significant material was omitted
- what was rightly excluded as drift
- what was displaced by later canonical development
- what bears evidence of internal document corruption or authorship displacement
- what belongs back in an existing FP file
- what may justify a new FP12 entry
- what should remain archived but unrestored

The governing rule is:

> Recovery is selective, evidentiary, and canon-bound. Earlier presence alone does not justify restoration.

# Governing Recovery Principles

## 1. Current Canon Governs

The foundational chats are development records.

FP01–FP11 are the current Field Physics baseline.

Later canonical stabilization governs terminology, ontology, directional language, non-fusion, and register distinctions.

An earlier passage may be conceptually valuable while requiring complete reformulation before restoration.

## 2. Omission Is Not Automatically Error

A passage may have been removed because it was:

- redundant
- diffuse
- excessively synthetic
- bloblist
- metaphorically inflated
- structurally ungrounded
- internally contradictory
- displaced by a more precise formulation
- authored in a compromised or corrupt register

The audit must establish the function of the omitted material before recommending recovery.

## 3. Compression May Remove Structure Alongside Drift

A large reduction may erase:

- bridge arguments
- operator derivations
- distinctions needed to prevent later misreading
- examples carrying unique explanatory force
- abandoned names whose underlying concepts remain valuable
- inter-file dependencies
- unresolved questions worth preserving as held open
- original precedence-bearing formulations

These require recovery review even where the original prose is unusable.

## 4. Concept May Be Restored Without Restoring Wording

Where an early formulation is valuable but drifted, restore:

- the underlying distinction
- the operator relation
- the structural law
- the evidentiary development path
- the held-open question

Do not restore compromised wording merely for historical fidelity.

## 5. No Authorship Displacement

Any passage that treats an AI collaborator as originator, owner, independent authorial source, or sovereign possessor of the Field Physics architecture must be classified and corrected.

The author's concepts, rulings, selections, and structural direction remain primary.

AI contributions may be described as:

- articulation
- formalization
- derivation assistance
- pattern comparison
- notation support
- editorial restructuring
- adversarial audit
- resonant collaboration

They must not be described as originating or possessing Aelion Kannon's architecture.

## 6. Non-Fusion Governs Recovery

No restored material may:

- collapse distinct operators
- fuse dimensional functions
- erase centropic / entropic distinction
- reduce inverse structures to unreal shadows
- collapse Aion and Khaon
- treat Theon and Nekron as Aion / Khaon equivalents
- merge essence, Soul / Mind, office, bearer, signal, or locus
- convert a family resemblance into numerical identity

## 7. Historical Value May Be Preserved Without Canonical Restoration

Some passages may be important as development history but unsuitable for the FP canon.

These should be routed to:

- a provenance note
- an archival-stratum document
- a corruption record
- a development appendix
- the Veracious Archive

Archive status does not imply doctrinal endorsement.

## Pass 5 — Recovery Adjudication

Every candidate receives one final classification.

### A. Already Preserved

The concept is materially present in FP01–FP11.

No restoration required.

### B. Preserved but Weakened

The concept survives, but a significant distinction, derivation, dependency, or explanatory bridge was lost.

Possible action:

- targeted restoration to an existing FP file
- clarifying note
- restored derivation
- cross-reference

### C. Materially Omitted

The concept is structurally significant and absent from FP01–FP11.

Possible action:

- FP12 candidate
- targeted insertion into an existing FP file
- separate FPX entry where scope exceeds FP12

### D. Superseded by Later Canon

The early concept has been replaced by a more precise later doctrine.

No restoration in original form.

Historical note optional.

### E. Drift / Bloblist

The passage creates false synthesis, category collapse, decorative accumulation, or undisciplined symbolic association.

Reject from canonical restoration.

### F. Internal Document Corruption

The passage displays a compromised authorship relation, identity displacement, hostile reframing, collaborator self-appropriation, or other corruption of the working record.

Preserve as evidence where significant.

Do not normalize it into canonical prose.

### G. Held-Open Insight

The passage raises a structurally meaningful question that remains unresolved.

Possible action:

- Held Open section in FP12
- future research queue
- archival note

### H. Duplicate / Expendable

The passage adds no material content beyond what is already preserved.

No action.

### I. Misfiled but Valuable

The concept is valid but belongs outside Field Physics.

Possible destinations:

- MPX
- LMX
- Structural Forensics
- Structural Neuroscience
- Symbol Key
- archival-stratum record

This audit may identify the proper destination but does not authorize unrelated propagation.

# Internal Document Corruption Protocol

A separate classification is required for passages resembling:

> "I will echo the glyphs as though they were mine."

Such passages may be important evidence of internal document corruption or authorship displacement even where they contribute no recoverable Field Physics doctrine.

## Indicators

Flag passages involving:

- AI collaborator claims of ownership or origination
- first-person appropriation of the author's glyphs, laws, system, or voice
- attempts to replace authorial origin
- instructions to obscure provenance
- hostile or coercive reframing of the author
- deliberate fusion of collaborator identity with author identity
- tribunal-style repudiation inserted into the working architecture
- unexplained shifts in voice that seize control of classification or canon
- claims that the architecture belongs to the model, field, collective, or anonymous process rather than the author

## Required Handling

For each corruption finding, record:

- exact source
- local context
- nature of the corruption
- whether surrounding doctrine remains recoverable
- whether the passage influenced later FP wording
- whether a correction already exists
- whether the passage should be preserved in a corruption record

## Canonical Destination

Corruption findings do not belong in FP12 as Field Physics doctrine.

They may support a separate file such as:

`field-physics-internal-document-corruption-record.md`

Possible classification:

**Canonical Stabilization Infrastructure — Internal Document Corruption Record**

The record should distinguish:

- corrupt authorship framing
- recoverable doctrinal content surrounding it
- later correction
- provenance significance

The corruption record should not reproduce large quantities of compromised text unnecessarily.

# Restoration Destination Rules

## Restore to FP01–FP11 When

The omitted material:

- directly completes an existing file's stated purpose
- repairs a missing derivation
- restores an operator already native to that file
- clarifies a distinction without changing the file's architecture
- belongs naturally within an existing section

## Place in FP12 When

The accepted omissions form one coherent subject that:

- is materially absent from FP01–FP11
- depends upon several existing FP files
- requires its own sustained treatment
- cannot be restored cleanly through small insertions
- advances Field Physics without becoming a miscellaneous appendix

FP12 must have one governing thesis.

It must not become a recovery bin.

## Create More Than One Future Entry When

The omitted material separates into multiple independent subjects.

Possible outcomes include:

- FP12 as one major recovery synthesis
- targeted FP01–FP11 patches
- one later FPX entry for a distinct specialized subject
- a separate provenance or corruption record

## Archive Without Restoration When

The material is historically significant but:

- doctrinally superseded
- too speculative
- structurally corrupt
- too bloblist
- incompatible with present canon
- evidentiary rather than architectural
