# Radical Skepticism

## Definition

**Radical Skepticism** is the Zenetist epistemic position that all perception, interpretation, and systems of knowledge remain conditionally mediated and therefore provisionally uncertain.

It holds that no narrative, framework, ideology, metaphysical system, scientific model, religious claim, esoteric system, or contra-esoteric critique can be treated as final or absolute, because every frame of understanding may itself exist within a larger and presently unseen frame.

Thus:

- perception is mediated
- interpretation is conditioned
- certainty is provisional
- finality-claims remain contestable

This principle applies equally to:

- religious systems
- scientific systems
- political systems
- esoteric systems
- contra-esoteric systems
- philosophical systems
- anti-metaphysical systems

Zenetist Radical Skepticism does not deny reality.

Rather, it denies the certainty that any interpretive structure has fully captured reality.

Its function is not negation.

Its function is structural honesty before the unknown.

---

## Origination of the Term

While the phrase "radical skepticism" has appeared in philosophical discourse as a general descriptor for strong skeptical positions, **Zenetist Radical Skepticism** names a specific formal articulation within the Zenetist framework.

It is not identical with Pyrrhonian skepticism, Cartesian methodological doubt, fallibilism, nihilism, relativism, or anti-metaphysical skepticism.

Those traditions may share structural adjacency with Zenetist Radical Skepticism, but they do not exhaust its meaning.

In Zenetist usage, Radical Skepticism names the epistemic posture that recognizes the conditionality of all frames of understanding, including the frame that claims to have overcome framing itself.

---

## Core Principle

> **There is adequate reason to doubt perception,**  
> **and the same reason to doubt all things perceived —**  
> **including the belief that one has transcended perception.**

This principle is self-applying.

It does not permit the skeptic to stand outside the field of mediation as though skepticism itself were final, purified, or exempt from doubt.

Radical Skepticism therefore prevents its own absolutization.

The claim "all perception is mediated" must itself remain subject to the same structural humility it requires of other claims.

---

## Range of Application

Radical Skepticism applies to every interpretive system that claims final access to reality.

It applies to religious systems when they claim exhaustive revelation.

It applies to scientific systems when they mistake methodological success for total ontological capture.

It applies to political systems when they convert partial social models into total explanations of human being.

It applies to esoteric systems when symbolic insight becomes dogmatic certainty.

It applies to contra-esoteric systems when critique of esotericism becomes its own closed ideology.

Radical Skepticism is therefore not anti-religious, anti-scientific, anti-esoteric, or anti-rational.

It is anti-finality.

---

## Distinction from Zenon

Radical Skepticism is structurally associated with **Zenon (🕳️)**, but it is not identical with Zenon.

**Radical Skepticism** is an epistemic posture.

It concerns the conditionality of knowledge-claims, perception, interpretation, and frame-construction.

**Zenon** is a metaphysical condition.

It names the Unknown Principle: the trans-structural ground beyond emanation, causality, awareness, polarity, and attribution.

Radical Skepticism recognizes that all frames of understanding remain provisional.

Zenon is what lies beyond all frames of understanding.

Thus, the two are related by their shared recognition of limit, but the limits are not the same kind.

Radical Skepticism names the limit of interpretive certainty.

Zenon names the trans-structural horizon beyond knowing-as-such.

Radical Skepticism does not reach Zenon.

It only prevents the mind from mistaking any frame for the final capture of what Zenon withholds.

---

## Distinction from Adjacent Skepticisms

### Contra Pyrrhonian Skepticism

Pyrrhonian skepticism suspends judgment to avoid dogmatic certainty and often aims at tranquility.

Zenetist Radical Skepticism is not primarily therapeutic.

It does not suspend judgment merely to attain calm, nor does it deny the possibility of meaningful structural discernment.

It holds that all frames remain conditionally mediated and therefore subject to larger unseen frames.

### Contra Fallibilism

Fallibilism holds that any particular belief or claim may be mistaken.

Radical Skepticism goes further.

It does not only question individual claims.

It questions the frame within which claims become intelligible.

A statement may be valid within one frame while the frame itself remains partial, conditioned, or embedded within a larger structure.

### Contra Nihilism and Relativism

Radical Skepticism does not imply that all claims are equally invalid.

It does not deny meaning, coherence, structure, or veracious insight.

It denies only finality.

Some frames may be more coherent, more structurally faithful, more generative, or more veracious than others.

But no frame may claim absolute closure over reality itself.

---

## Structural Function

Radical Skepticism protects Zenetism from becoming dogmatic about its own architecture.

It requires that every system, including Zenetism, remain aware of its mediated status.

This does not weaken structural metaphysics.

It stabilizes it.

A framework that cannot question its own limits becomes ideology.

A framework that can preserve structure while remaining open before the unknown retains coherence without absolutization.

Radical Skepticism therefore functions as an epistemic safeguard.

It permits conviction without finality.

It permits structure without closure.

It permits discernment without totalization.

## Summary

Radical Skepticism is not denial.

It is not nihilism.

It is not relativistic flattening.

It is the disciplined recognition that every frame of understanding remains conditional, mediated, and potentially nested within a larger unseen frame.

It does not destroy knowledge.

It prevents knowledge from mistaking itself for finality.

**Radical Skepticism is the refusal to let any frame call itself complete before the Unknown.**
