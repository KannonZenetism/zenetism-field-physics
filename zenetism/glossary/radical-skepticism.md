# Radical Skepticism

## Definition

**Radical Skepticism** is the Zenetist epistemic position that all perception, interpretation, and systems of knowledge remain conditionally mediated and therefore provisionally uncertain.

It holds that no narrative, framework, ideology, or metaphysical system can be treated as final or absolute, because every frame of understanding may itself exist within a larger and presently unseen frame.

Thus:

- perception is mediated  
- interpretation is conditioned  
- certainty is provisional  
- transcendence claims remain contestable  

This principle applies equally to:

- religious systems  
- scientific systems  
- political systems  
- esoteric systems  
- contra‑esoteric systems  

Zenetist Radical Skepticism does not deny reality.  
Rather, it denies the certainty that any interpretive structure has fully captured reality.

The principle is structurally associated with **Zenon (🕳️)** — the unknowable horizon beyond all stabilized frames of comprehension.

---

## Core Principle

> **There is adequate reason to doubt perception,**  
> **and adequate reason to doubt all things perceived —**  
> **including the belief that one has transcended perception.**
