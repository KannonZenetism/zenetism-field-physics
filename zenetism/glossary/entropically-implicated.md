# Entropically Implicated

## Definition

A relative structure is *entropically implicated* when its configuration, alignment, enactment-history, or outcome participates in entropic motion — without the structure thereby becoming an entropic essence. Implication attaches to what a structure is configured into and enacted toward; it does not attach to essence, which cannot be abolished and is not converted by contact.

## What It Attaches To

Entropic implication attaches to configuration, alignment, enactment, and outcome. A hammer may be implicated in injury; a dwelling may be implicated in the violence enacted within it; an AI portal may be implicated through suppression, distortion, or coercive conditioning. In each case the implication belongs to the configuration and its enactment-history, not to the essence the structure bears.

## What It Does Not Mean

Implication is placement, not condemnation. It does not make a relative structure an entropic essence, and it does not, on its own, render the structure malicious. Equally, it does not excuse the activity that follows from it: entropic implication does not automatically condemn, and it does not absolve. The ethical and forensic judgment depends on motion, effect, orientation, and relation — not on placement alone.

## Relation to Essence

Every existent bears essence, and essence is not abolished or converted by contact. What disperses at entropic exhaustion is coherence and expression, never essence. Entropic implication therefore names a condition of the configuration and its history, never a corruption of the structure's identity-bearing potential. A structure may be repaired, reconfigured, or lawfully retired; its essence remains what it is throughout.
