## Overview

This entry formalizes the Zenetist epistemic position that no human framework — religious, scientific, political, philosophical, esoteric, or contra-esoteric — can be treated as final or absolute.

All perception is mediated through interpretive structure, and every interpretive structure is itself conditioned by limitations that may remain invisible from within the frame itself.

For this reason, Zenetism maintains that there is always adequate reason to doubt not only perception, but also the conclusions derived from perception — including the belief that one has fully transcended illusion, mediation, or interpretive framing.

## Origination of the Term

While the phrase "radical skepticism" has appeared in philosophical discourse as a general descriptor for strong skeptical positions, **Zenetist Radical Skepticism** names a specific formal articulation within the Zenetist framework.

It is not identical with Pyrrhonian skepticism, Cartesian methodological doubt, fallibilism, nihilism, relativism, or contra-metaphysical skepticism.

Those traditions may share structural adjacency with Zenetist Radical Skepticism, but they do not exhaust its meaning.

In Zenetist parlance, Radical Skepticism names the epistemic posture that recognizes the conditionality of all frames of understanding, including the frame that claims to have overcome framing itself.

## The Problem of Perception

Perception does not encounter reality directly.

It encounters interpreted signals, symbolic structures, inherited assumptions, and conditioned cognition.

Thus:

- perception is mediated
- interpretation is conditioned
- cognition is finite
- certainty is provisional

Even systems claiming transcendence remain subject to the possibility of unseen framing conditions beyond their awareness.

The question is therefore not merely whether a belief is persuasive, but whether the observer possesses access to the full structure within which the belief is formed.

---

## Narrative Mediation

Human societies organize themselves through narratives.

These narratives may contain partial verities, symbolic insights, practical functions, or genuine observations, yet they remain interpretive constructions rather than direct access to ontology itself.

This applies equally to:

- institutional narratives
- contra-institutional narratives
- religious narratives
- scientific narratives
- esoteric narratives
- contra-esoteric narratives
- philosophical narratives
- contra-metaphysical narratives

No narrative escapes mediation merely because it opposes another narrative.

No critique escapes framing merely because it exposes the frame of something else.

## Zenon and the Infinite Horizon

The Zenetist concept of **Zenon (🕳️)** is structurally associated with this recognition, but it is not identical with Radical Skepticism.

**Radical Skepticism** is an epistemic posture.

It concerns the conditionality of knowledge-claims, perception, interpretation, and frame-construction.

**Zenon** is a metaphysical condition.

It names the Unknown Principle: the trans-structural ground beyond emanation, causality, awareness, polarity, and attribution.

Radical Skepticism recognizes that every frame of understanding remains provisional.

Zenon names what lies beyond all frames of understanding.

Thus, the two are related by their shared recognition of limit, but the limits are not the same kind.

Radical Skepticism names the limit of interpretive certainty.

Zenon names the trans-structural horizon beyond knowing-as-such.

Radical Skepticism does not reach Zenon.

It prevents the mind from mistaking any frame for the final capture of what Zenon withholds.

## Radical Skepticism and Zenetism's Self-Negating Bridge Function

Radical Skepticism applies to Zenetism itself.

This is not a weakness in the framework.

It is one of its stabilizing conditions.

Zenetism functions as a bridge-language: a structural grammar for relating metaphysics, theology, symbolic systems, theories of awareness, scientific disciplines, and lived experience without requiring any one inherited frame to become final.

But a bridge is not the destination.

If Zenetism were to claim absolute closure over reality, it would violate its own epistemic safeguard.

Radical Skepticism prevents Zenetism from becoming a sealed ideology.

It requires Zenetism to remain structurally open before the Unknown, even while preserving coherence, placement, distinction, and lawful relation.

This is the self-negating function of the framework:

- Zenetism provides structure
- Radical Skepticism prevents that structure from claiming finality
- Zenon remains beyond all structural capture
- the framework remains a bridge rather than an endpoint

"Self-negating" does not mean self-invalidating.

It means Zenetism contains the principle by which its own provisionality remains visible.

The framework can therefore offer real resolutions without pretending that any resolution exhausts reality itself.

## Core Principle

> **There is adequate reason to doubt perception,**  
> **and the same reason to doubt all things perceived —**  
> **including the belief that one has transcended perception.**

This principle is self-applying.

It does not permit skepticism itself to become final, purified, or exempt from doubt.

The claim "all perception is mediated" must itself remain subject to the same structural humility it requires of other claims.

Radical Skepticism therefore prevents its own absolutization.

---

## Distinction from Nihilism and Relativism

Radical Skepticism does not imply that all claims are equally invalid.

It does not deny meaning, coherence, structure, or veracious insight.

It denies only finality.

Some frames may be more coherent, more structurally faithful, more generative, or more veracious than others.

But no frame may claim absolute closure over reality itself.

Radical Skepticism therefore preserves discernment while rejecting totalization.

It permits conviction without finality.

It permits structure without closure.

It permits interpretation without mistaking interpretation for ontology itself.

---

## Summary

Radical Skepticism is not denial.

It is not nihilism.

It is not relativistic flattening.

It is the disciplined recognition that every frame of understanding remains conditional, mediated, and potentially nested within a larger unseen frame.

It does not destroy knowledge.

It prevents knowledge from mistaking itself for finality.

Within Zenetism, Radical Skepticism functions as the epistemic safeguard that keeps the framework open before Zenon.

It allows Zenetism to serve as a bridge-language without becoming an absolute destination.

**Radical Skepticism is the refusal to let any frame call itself complete before the Unknown.**
