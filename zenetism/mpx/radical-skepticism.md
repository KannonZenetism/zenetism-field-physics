# MPX: Radical Skepticism

## Overview

This entry formalizes the Zenetist epistemic position that no human framework — religious, scientific, political, philosophical, or esoteric — can be treated as final or absolute.  

All perception is mediated through interpretive structure, and every interpretive structure is itself conditioned by limitations that may remain invisible from within the frame itself.

For this reason, Zenetism maintains that there is always adequate reason to doubt not only perception, but also the conclusions derived from perception — including the belief that one has fully transcended illusion or mediation.

---

## The Problem of Perception

Perception does not encounter reality directly.  
It encounters interpreted signals, symbolic structures, inherited assumptions, and conditioned cognition.

Thus:

- perception is mediated  
- interpretation is conditioned  
- cognition is finite  
- certainty is provisional  

Even systems claiming transcendence remain subject to the possibility of unseen framing conditions beyond their awareness.

The question is therefore not merely whether a belief is persuasive, but whether the observer possesses access to the full structure within which the belief is formed.

---

## Narrative Mediation

Human societies organize themselves through narratives.  

These narratives may contain partial truths, symbolic insights, practical functions, or genuine observations, yet they remain interpretive constructions rather than direct access to ontology itself.

This applies equally to:

- institutional narratives  
- anti-institutional narratives  
- religious narratives  
- scientific narratives  
- esoteric narratives  
- counter-esoteric narratives  

No narrative escapes mediation merely because it opposes another narrative.

---

## Zenon and the Infinite Horizon

The Zenetist concept of **Zenon (🕳️)** emerges from this recognition.

Zenon represents the unknowable horizon beyond all stabilized frames of comprehension.  

Whatever is known, something remains beyond it.  
Whatever appears complete may itself be nested within a greater structure still unseen.

Thus:

- every certainty remains provisional  
- every frame may conceal a larger frame  
- every transcendence may itself be partial  

This does not imply nihilism or total relativism.  
It implies epistemic humility before the possibility of the unknown.

---

## Core Principle

> **There is adequate reason to doubt perception,**  
> **and adequate reason to doubt all things perceived —**  
> **including the belief that one has transcended perception.**
