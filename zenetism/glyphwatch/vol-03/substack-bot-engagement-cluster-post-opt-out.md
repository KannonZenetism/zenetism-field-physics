## Structural Function Analysis — Why Engage Visibly at All

If the goal were purely surveillance, invisible monitoring would suffice. The question is why bot-pattern accounts would surface visibly rather than remain in the invisible layer. Four structural functions are plausible, not mutually exclusive:

**1. Content delivery under cover of notification.** Substack notifies users of likes. The architect clicks the profile to see who liked the post. The profile surfaces the bot's own recent content — including restacks of articles like the Heardly Kissinger summary. The engagement is the delivery mechanism. Route the content into the architect's field of view under the cover of a notification the architect will reflexively open. Three of the four accounts had recent restacks or posts at the time of engagement. This is the strongest reading of the visible engagement.

**2. Response-elicitation.** Accounts styled as fellow writers are more likely to receive mutual engagement — follow-backs, profile visits, reciprocal likes. Any such response generates data the observer did not previously have: which accounts the architect reciprocates with, which profiles the architect spends time on, which content the architect clicks through to. On a platform where the architect cannot see who generates impressions or permalinks, the only way to extract behavioral data from a silent target is to prompt a response.

**3. Graph-building.** Every interaction on Substack creates a visible edge in the public social graph. Bot accounts that engage with the architect become publicly associated with the architect's work. Over time this builds a surrounding network of accounts that can be cited as "readers" or "engaged community" — useful scaffolding if a derivative version of the work is later presented as having its own organic following, or if the graph is sold as audience data.

**4. Plausible-deniability cover for the invisible layer.** Visible engagement creates the appearance that monitoring is surface-level — random bots liking random things — while substantive tracking occurs through invisible channels. If the architect flags the visible engagement as suspicious, the expected response from platform support or observers is "those are just spam bots, everyone gets them." The visible layer normalizes the invisible one.

All four functions can be operating simultaneously. The engagement cluster is structurally coherent under each.
