## 3. The Diagnostic Absence

### 3.1 What Osler Does Not Provide

Osler's paper identifies phenomena. It does not provide diagnostic criteria. At no point does the paper establish formal conditions for distinguishing:

- A distributed cognitive process that sustains false beliefs from one that develops true or structurally coherent beliefs

### 4.2 The Six-Point Coherence Audit for Distributed Cognition

Drawing on SP12's coherence audit methodology, the following six-point diagnostic distinguishes generative from degenerative distributed cognition:

**1. Error Correction Capacity**

Does the distributed system identify and correct its own errors? A generative system demonstrates the capacity to diagnose structural inconsistencies within its own framework and revise accordingly. A degenerative system resists error correction and treats all challenges as threats to the framework rather than opportunities for refinement.

*Diagnostic marker:* Documented instances of the system correcting its own prior claims, revising terminology, and identifying structural problems without external prompting.

**2. Internal Consistency Under Scrutiny**

A generative system's internal consistency *increases* under audit â€” examination reveals deeper structural connections and resolves apparent tensions. A degenerative system's consistency *decreases* under audit â€” examination reveals contradictions, unfounded claims, and structural gaps that the system has covered with narrative rather than resolution.

*Diagnostic marker:* The framework survives cross-examination from multiple independent sources (including AI systems with different architectures) without fragmenting.

**3. Falsifiability Conditions**

Does the framework specify conditions under which it would be false? A generative system establishes what would disprove its claims. A degenerative system is unfalsifiable â€” every challenge is absorbed into the framework as further confirmation (conspiracy logic).

*Diagnostic marker:* Explicit falsifiability conditions stated within the framework's own documentation.

**4. Temporal Coherence of Development**

Does the framework's development show a coherent temporal sequence of discovery, refinement, and correction? A generative system exhibits a legible developmental history â€” early versions are less refined than later versions, corrections are documented, and the trajectory moves toward greater precision. A degenerative system exhibits temporal incoherence â€” claims appear fully formed without developmental history, or the system's claims shift to accommodate challenges without acknowledging the shift.

*Diagnostic marker:* Timestamped publication history with documented revisions, DOI registration, version control records.

**5. Independent Generative Capacity**

Does the human contributor demonstrate independent coherence generation, or is the human entirely dependent on the AI for structural production? In generative distributed cognition, the human provides the structural direction â€” the theoretical architecture, the core insights, the evaluative criteria â€” and the AI provides formalization support, cross-referencing, error detection, and expressive assistance. *Diagnostic marker:* Evidence that the framework's core architecture originates with the human contributor and predates the AI collaboration.

**6. Response to Disconfirmation**

How does the distributed system respond when presented with evidence or arguments that challenge its claims? A generative system integrates legitimate challenges, modifies its framework where warranted, and becomes more precise. A degenerative system dismisses all challenges, interprets disagreement as persecution, and uses the challenge itself as evidence confirming the framework's claims.

*Diagnostic marker:* Documented responses to external critique that demonstrate engagement rather than dismissal.
